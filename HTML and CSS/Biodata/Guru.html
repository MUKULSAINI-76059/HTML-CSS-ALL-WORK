Dhirendra Krishna Shastri

Born on July 4, 1996,

Dhirendra Krishna Shastri, popularly known as Bageshwar Dham Sarkar, was born on 4th July 1996 in Gadha village of Chhatarpur district, Madhya Pradesh, India. He belongs to a simple Brahmin family, where his father, Ram Karpal Garg, and mother, Saroj Garg, raised him with traditional Hindu values. From a very young age, he was inclined towards spirituality and devoted his life to religious practices and service to society. He pursued his basic education in Chhatarpur but later dedicated most of his time to spiritual learning and preaching.

Today, Dhirendra Krishna Shastri is the Peethadhish (head) of Bageshwar Dham, a famous pilgrimage site known for faith, devotion, and divine experiences. He is widely recognized for organizing ‘Divya Darbars’ where he narrates stories from Sanatan Dharma and guides people on the path of spirituality and humanity. He is admired for his eloquent discourses, social work, and his mission to promote Indian culture, dharma, and values among the younger generation. His followers believe him to be blessed with divine powers, and he continues to inspire millions of devotees across India and abroad.
